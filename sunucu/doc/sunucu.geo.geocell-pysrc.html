 17  """Defines the notion of 'geocells' and exposes methods to operate on them. 
 18   
 19  A geocell is a hexadecimal string that defines a two dimensional rectangular 
 20  region inside the [-90,90] x [-180,180] latitude/longitude space. A geocell's 
 21  'resolution' is its length. For most practical purposes, at high resolutions, 
 22  geocells can be treated as single points. 
 23   
 24  Much like geohashes (see http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geohash), geocells are 
 25  hierarchical, in that any prefix of a geocell is considered its ancestor, with 
 26  geocell[:-1] being geocell's immediate parent cell. 
 27   
 28  To calculate the rectangle of a given geocell string, first divide the 
 29  [-90,90] x [-180,180] latitude/longitude space evenly into a 4x4 grid like so: 
 30   
 31               +---+---+---+---+ (90, 180) 
 32               | a | b | e | f | 
 33               +---+---+---+---+ 
 34               | 8 | 9 | c | d | 
 35               +---+---+---+---+ 
 36               | 2 | 3 | 6 | 7 | 
 37               +---+---+---+---+ 
 38               | 0 | 1 | 4 | 5 | 
 39    (-90,-180) +---+---+---+---+ 
 40   
 41  NOTE: The point (0, 0) is at the intersection of grid cells 3, 6, 9 and c. And, 
 42        for example, cell 7 should be the sub-rectangle from 
 43        (-45, 90) to (0, 180). 
 44   
 45  Calculate the sub-rectangle for the first character of the geocell string and 
 46  re-divide this sub-rectangle into another 4x4 grid. For example, if the geocell 
 47  string is '78a', we will re-divide the sub-rectangle like so: 
 48   
 49                 .                   . 
 50                 .                   . 
 51             . . +----+----+----+----+ (0, 180) 
 52                 | 7a | 7b | 7e | 7f | 
 53                 +----+----+----+----+ 
 54                 | 78 | 79 | 7c | 7d | 
 55                 +----+----+----+----+ 
 56                 | 72 | 73 | 76 | 77 | 
 57                 +----+----+----+----+ 
 58                 | 70 | 71 | 74 | 75 | 
 59    . . (-45,90) +----+----+----+----+ 
 60                 .                   . 
 61                 .                   . 
 62   
 63  Continue to re-divide into sub-rectangles and 4x4 grids until the entire 
 64  geocell string has been exhausted. The final sub-rectangle is the rectangular 
 65  region for the geocell. 
 66  """
